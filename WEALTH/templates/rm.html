Risk Management

Utilizing proper risk management techniques can help you limit your downside potential while also maximizing gains. By allocating atleast 5% of your portfolio to a short position and adjusting it based on market conditions, it is possible to considerably hedge against market corrections while outperforming the S&P 500 at the same time. This portfolio is comprised of 95% stocks and 5% in SQQQ, which is a 3x leveraged ETF that shorts the NASDAQ. By holding SQQQ, this portfolio was able to withstand the intense market volatility in Q4 of 2021, as seen in the graph below.
From peak to trough, the SPY fell around 20% in 2021. However, this portfolio only dropped 5%, mainly due to holding SQQQ and with an adjustive strategy that increases or decreases the portfolio's holdings of stocks depending on market conditions. This portfolio consists of the same stocks as above, but without any holdings of SQQQ and without the use of the adjustive strategy. This presents a much higher risk exposure which makes the portfolio vulnerable to steep declines in returns. From peak to trough, this portfolio dropped 12%, a much more significant decrease from the portfolio above. Although this portfolio does outperform the one above, the additional risk should be accounted for.
